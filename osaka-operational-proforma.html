This operational pro-forma covers the full 12-month lifecycle from acquisition through stabilised operation for the Ebisuhigashi 1LDK proxy unit in Naniwa Ward, Osaka — the highest-conviction site from the Osaka STR Feasibility Model (March 2026). Total capital required is ¥46,750,000, comprising the ¥42,000,000 purchase price, ¥3,150,000 in transaction costs (7.5%), ¥800,000 in STR-grade fit-out and smart-lock installation, ¥350,000 in pre-opening compliance and licensing transfer fees, and a ¥450,000 operating cash reserve held at Month 0 to cover the zero-revenue pre-opening period (Months 1–3).

Under the base-case scenario (65% annual average occupancy, ¥14,000 ADR, seasonal tiering applied), the cumulative cash position at Month 12 reaches +¥692,766 — representing recovery of the initial operating reserve and accumulation of the first meaningful cash surplus. The property reaches cash-flow breakeven in Month 6 on a cumulative basis as pre-opening costs are absorbed by building revenue. Under the stress case (45% occupancy, ADR compressed −15% to ¥11,900), the Month 12 cumulative position is −¥318,440 — a controlled drawdown within the operating reserve, not a capital-destruction event, given the all-cash structure carries no debt service obligation.

The breakeven occupancy on a monthly NOI basis is 36% (covering all operating costs excluding the one-time startup items). The leveraged structure breakeven was 57%, which is why all-cash is the recommended structure pending Cabinet Office regulatory resolution.

The single biggest operational risk is complaint-driven enforcement action in Naniwa Ward, given its status as Osaka's highest-density STR micro-market. Mitigation is operational, not structural: execute a guest screening protocol that filters group bookings above 4 guests for the 35m² unit size, install noise monitoring hardware (SoundPrint or equivalent, ¥45,000), and maintain a documented 30-minute response SLA with the kanrisha for all noise complaints. The management company must be contracted with an explicit complaint-response clause — any management company failing to respond within 30 minutes to a verified noise complaint is grounds for immediate contract termination. This is the non-negotiable operational requirement that converts Naniwa Ward's enforcement risk from existential to manageable.

Stress Case Interpretation
The stress case (45% average occupancy, ADR −15%) produces a Month 12 cumulative position of −¥318,440 against the ¥450,000 operating reserve — meaning the reserve is partially depleted but not fully consumed. The property does not produce a capital-destruction event under all-cash structure; it produces a controlled first-year cash loss of approximately ¥318K that is absorbed by the pre-funded reserve. The critical implication: the all-cash structure turns a stress scenario into a manageable working capital event. The same scenario under the leveraged structure would produce −¥1.6M in cumulative cash (stress NOI of ¥294K minus ¥1.28M debt service), requiring a capital injection that would exceed the initial operating reserve.

Critical-Path Timeline — Acquisition to First Guest

The critical path runs approximately 90 days from purchase agreement execution to first guest check-in. The binding constraint is the Special Zone licence transfer process, which requires a city filing, site inspection, and approximately 30–45 days for approval. Fire safety recertification after ownership change is a parallel track. The listing optimisation period (4–6 weeks of being live without reviews before the algorithm begins promoting the listing) means Month 4 launch is the realistic earliest date for approaching full-tier occupancy rates.
