05

Recommendations & Calibration Thresholds

The following six recommendations are derived from the simulation matrix and annotated examples. Each recommendation includes a named numeric threshold that can be encoded directly into the regime gate logic or position-sizing rules. These are proposed refinements to HMRRS v1.0 and are flagged for inclusion in a v1.1 update cycle.

R.01
Introduce ETH R2 Long Minimum Momentum Threshold
The ETH R2 Long bucket is the weakest cell in the matrix: 47.1% win rate and +0.12R average R-multiple at 0.5× sizing produces near-zero expected value. The simulation suggests this is not a regime gating failure but a signal quality failure — marginal momentum signals that pass the gate on soft criteria. Proposed fix: require 4H close momentum to exceed 0.65σ above the 20-period EMA before a long entry is permitted in R2 on ETH. This threshold filters approximately 34% of ETH R2 long signals, with simulated improvement to 54% win rate and +0.41R average R-multiple on the surviving set.
R.02
Add R1/R2 Boundary Confidence Score — Long Entry Guard
When the six-gate cascade yields R1 but two or more gates are within 10% of their R2 threshold values, classify the period as "R1-Marginal" and apply a secondary confirmation requirement before permitting long entries. The secondary confirmation is: Gate 4 (correlation filter) must show BTC-ETH 4H correlation above 0.72 for ETH longs, or the 24H spot volume ratio must be above 1.15× its 7-day average for BTC longs. Short entries at R1-Marginal are unaffected (they receive full 1.0× sizing under R2 rules regardless). This addresses EX-03 and prevents overconfident long sizing at regime boundaries.
R.03
Formalise Post-R3 Re-entry Cool-down: 72-Hour Hold Rule
Following any R3 classification, the simulation shows that the first 72 hours of subsequent R1 or R2 signals carry a false signal rate of 28.4%, versus 13.1% baseline. The proposed rule: after any R3 classification, suppress all long entries for 72 hours (18 × 4H bars). Short entries remain eligible if regime reclassifies to R1 or R2 within this window, subject to standard gate requirements. This prevents premature recovery trades during what the edge-case manual calls the "volatility echo" period following stress events. The 72-hour figure is derived from the point at which the post-R3 false signal rate falls back within one standard deviation of the baseline.
R.04
R2 Short — Retain Full Sizing, Flag OI Contraction Risk
The R2 Short bucket (BTC 61.1% win rate / +0.68R; ETH 57.1% / +0.55R) outperforms the R2 Long bucket significantly and justifies the asymmetric sizing rule as implemented. However, the simulation identifies that R2 short false signals are disproportionately clustered during periods of OI contraction — specifically when 4H OI change is below −2.5% (forced liquidation-driven price drop rather than genuine momentum). Proposed addition: within R2 short entries, flag and reduce to 0.75× sizing when the 4H OI change crosses below −2.5%, to avoid chasing liquidation wicks.
R.05
R1 BTC — Sustained Outperformance Warrants Incremental Sizing Study
The R1 BTC Long/Short combined bucket is the highest-performing cell in the matrix: 67.6% win rate, +1.21R average, 8.8% false signal rate. This outperformance is consistent across all three walk-forward folds (fold variance: ±0.14R). The current 1.0× sizing cap may be conservative for this cell. A proposed study: introduce a conditional R1-BTC sizing increment to 1.25× BASE_RISK_UNIT when at least 3 consecutive 4H bars have confirmed R1 classification (regime persistence signal). Cap total portfolio R1-BTC exposure at 2× BASE_RISK_UNIT per open position to prevent over-concentration. This is a paper-trade study item for v1.1 — no live size increase until the forward-test confirms fold-stability.
R.06
Define Formal R4 Exit Criteria and Re-entry Protocol
R4 events in the simulation were brief but severe (2–4 day clusters). The current specification suppresses all entries during R4 but does not define the re-entry protocol after R4 resolves. Proposed: R4 exits when all six gates simultaneously clear their R3 thresholds (not merely R4 → R3 reclassification). Upon R4 exit, apply the 72-hour hold rule from R.03 before permitting any long entries. Short entries may be permitted after 24 hours post-R4 exit if Gate 1C-i (liquidation proximity) is clear and the 4H close shows a new momentum signal. This prevents the most dangerous re-entry scenario: buying into a temporary R4 recovery that subsequently continues lower.
06

Simulation Limitations & Known Gaps

This audit is based on a backtested walk-forward simulation, not live system performance. Several limitations apply and should be disclosed before any of the above recommendations are implemented in a live environment.

The 84-day simulation window captures one market regime transition sequence and two acute stress periods, but does not cover sustained bear market conditions (prolonged R3/R4 dominance) or a full macro cycle. The regime prevalence distribution — 38% R1, 31% R2 — may not be representative of all market conditions, and recommendations derived from it may be over-fitted to a medium-term trending environment.

The BASE_RISK_UNIT of $570 was held constant throughout the simulation. In live deployment, this figure would need dynamic recalibration as account equity changes, and the regime-gated sizing rules would produce different absolute dollar outcomes at different capital scales. R-multiple analysis is invariant to this, but absolute expected value calculations are not.

Gate thresholds (funding rate ceiling at 0.020%/8h, OI expansion ratio floors, volatility ceilings) were calibrated on historical data included in the same general window used for backtest validation. True out-of-sample performance against novel regime configurations is unknown until forward-tested. All six recommendations above are proposed as paper-trade verification items before live implementation.
